## 1. Vision & Pillars

### The Elevator Pitch
**Minds** is a mind-bending, emergent simulation where the world reshapes itself based on how you *think*, *feel*, and *commit*. Every playthrough is different because the game's reality is a mirror of the player's internal choices — not just their button presses.

### Core Parable
> You create your own reality through directed focus, emotional commitment, and diligent effort.

This isn't just story dressing. It's the *central mechanic*. The game systems literally encode this idea: what you pay attention to grows, what you invest emotion in becomes vivid, and what you work at persistently transforms.

### Three Pillars

| Pillar | Meaning | Mechanical Expression |
|--------|---------|----------------------|
| **Directed Focus** | Attention shapes reality | A perception system — what you observe, examine, and name becomes more real and interactive in the world |
| **Emotional Commitment** | Feeling fuels change | An emotional resonance system — the intensity and type of emotional investment you bring to encounters determines how deeply the world responds |
| **Diligent Effort** | Persistence transforms | A practice/mastery loop — repeated, intentional action in a domain causes compounding, non-linear breakthroughs |

### Sub-Theme: Consciousness
The game quietly explores what consciousness *is* — through the player's experience of it. Are you the observer? The decider? Both? Neither? This never lectures; it emerges through play. Touchstones: panpsychism, the hard problem, qualia, Jungian archetypes, integrated information theory, neuroplasticity, the observer effect.

## 2. Philosophical Foundations

The game's systems aren't just fun mechanics with a consciousness "skin." Each one encodes a real idea from philosophy of mind, so that players who dig deep enough discover they've been *doing* philosophy the whole time.

### 2.1 The Observer Effect — "Observation Creates Reality"

**The idea:** In quantum mechanics, the double-slit experiment shows that particles behave as waves of probability until measured — at which point they "collapse" into definite states. The philosophical implications are provocative: does the presence of an observer play a role in determining reality?

**In the game:** This is the Perception System, made literal. Entities exist in a state of "quantum fog" until the player observes them. Observation collapses potential into form.

**Discovery mechanic — "The Double Slit":** An in-game passage with two paths. Approaching without focusing on either, the paths behave fluidly. The moment the player fixes their gaze on one, the other changes. You can never observe both simultaneously.

### 2.2 The Hard Problem — "Why Is There Experience?"

**The idea:** David Chalmers distinguished the "easy problems" of consciousness from the "hard problem": why is processing accompanied by subjective experience at all? Why does seeing red *feel* like something?

**In the game:** The Emotion System is the hard problem made playable. The game never labels emotions. The world changes — colors shift, sounds deepen, physics soften — and the player *feels* something. Is that feeling in the world, or in you?

**Discovery mechanic — "Mary's Room":** An in-game region is entirely monochrome. The player can learn everything *about* color. When they finally cross a threshold and color floods in, the experience is overwhelming — knowing about something is fundamentally different from experiencing it.

### 2.3 Qualia — "What Is It Like to Be?"

**The idea:** Thomas Nagel's "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" argues that conscious experience is inherently subjective. We can learn everything physical about bat echolocation, but never know what it *feels like* for a bat.

**In the game:** Entities have their own modes of perception. A crystal "sees" through vibration. The player must *resonate* with entities to experience traces of another mode of being. This is always incomplete — that incompleteness is the point.

**Discovery mechanic — "The Bat's Echo":** An entity communicates through a non-visual modality. The player must close their "eyes" to begin perceiving this entity's world. Alien, disorienting, beautiful — and untranslatable when vision returns.

### 2.4 Integrated Information Theory — "Consciousness as Connectedness"

**The idea:** Giulio Tononi's IIT proposes that consciousness arises from integrated information (Φ). A system is conscious to the degree that its parts produce more information together than separately.

**In the game:** The Mastery System's synergy mechanic. Individual domains are useful alone, but connections *between* domains produce qualitatively new emergence — greater than the sum of parts. This also drives the Seed Network's Φ threshold (see Section 3.3G).

**Discovery mechanic — "Phi":** A collection of inert objects. Arranging them in feedback-loop configurations causes emergent behavior — the collection exhibits something like awareness. Removing one element shatters the whole.

### 2.5 The Collective Unconscious — "Shared Patterns Across Minds"

**The idea:** Carl Jung proposed a collective unconscious — shared archetypes (Shadow, Anima/Animus, Wise Old Man) that appear across cultures. Also synchronicity: meaningful coincidences carrying psychological significance.

**In the game:** The backbone of cross-seed persistence (Section 3.3F). Deeply bonded entities become archetypes persisting across seeds. Synchronicity emerges from the collision of player choices and emergent world behavior.

**Discovery mechanic — "The Shadow":** An entity mirrors the player's behavior — inverted. Where they focused, it ignored. Confronting it (observing, resonating, understanding — not fighting) is one of the deepest realization paths.

### 2.6 Panpsychism — "Mind Is Fundamental"

**The idea:** Consciousness isn't emergent from complex physical systems — it's a fundamental feature of reality, present in everything. Everything has some form of inner life.

**In the game:** The world is built on this premise. Observation levels represent the degree to which you're attuning to inner life that was always there. You don't create consciousness by observing; you *recognize* it.

**Discovery mechanic — "The Listening Stone":** A seemingly inert rock. A player who gives it sustained, quiet attention will gradually perceive: the rock has been observing *them*. Its observation level ticks up on its own when the player is near.

### 2.7 Neuroplasticity — "Attention Physically Changes You"

**The idea:** Sustained mindfulness practice physically alters brain structure. Directed attention literally rewires the brain — increased cortical thickness, reduced amygdala reactivity, stronger white-matter connections.

**In the game:** Repeated, intentional practice changes what the player *can perceive*. A player with mastery in "listening" hears sounds that were always present but imperceptible. The world doesn't change; the player's capacity does.

**Discovery mechanic — "The Frequency":** A persistent, subtle sound runs through the entire game from the first frame. New players can't hear it. Mastery in sonic domains gradually reveals it — feeling, then hum, then complex pattern. The mastery didn't add the sound; it added the ears.

### 2.8 Jungian Individuation — "Becoming Whole"

**The idea:** Jung's individuation is the lifelong process of integrating conscious and unconscious aspects of the psyche — confronting the Shadow, recognizing the Anima/Animus, achieving relationship with the Self. Active imagination — dialoguing with inner figures — was his primary method.

**In the game:** The "Awakening" story arc. Realizations are recognitions of the player's own consciousness reflected in the world. The Mirror entity is an active imagination tool. The "ending" is a moment of integration.

### 2.9 Mechanic Summary Table

| Philosophical Concept | Game System | Discovery Mechanic | Cross-Seed Connection |
|---|---|---|---|
| Observer Effect | Perception | "The Double Slit" | Observation patterns carry across seeds |
| Hard Problem | Emotion | "Mary's Room" | Cumulative emotional signature persists |
| Qualia | Emotion + Perception | "The Bat's Echo" | Bonded entities retain their unique "feel" |
| Integrated Information Theory | Mastery synergies + Seed Network | "Phi" | Cross-domain mastery echoes; Φ threshold |
| Collective Unconscious | Cross-Seed Persistence | "The Shadow" | The entire persistence layer |
| Panpsychism | Perception (reversed) | "The Listening Stone" | Bonded entities gain independent observation |
| Neuroplasticity | Mastery | "The Frequency" | Meta-mastery profile carries across seeds |
| Individuation | Narrative (Awakening) | "The Shadow" | Shadow evolves based on all prior playthroughs |
| Emergence / Neural Networks | Seed Network + Φ | "The Awakening" | All seeds are nodes in a mind the player built |

## 3. Technical Architecture

### 3.1 Platform Decision: Web-First (TypeScript + WebGL)

| Layer | Technology | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Runtime | TypeScript | Type safety for complex systems, huge ecosystem |
| Renderer | Three.js | 3D first-person, runs in browser, exportable to desktop via Electron/Tauri |
| ECS Framework | Custom ("Mindcore") | Rolled our own — simpler, fully serializable, tight event bus integration |
| State Management | Custom event bus + immutable state | Enables save/load, time manipulation, mod inspection |
| Audio | Web Audio API (custom wrapper) | Spatial HRTF, procedural tones, ambient layers. No external audio libs. |
| Build | Vite | Fast dev iteration |
| Desktop wrapper | Tauri (later) | Lightweight native shell when ready |

**Why web-first:** Zero-friction distribution, instant sharing of mods/stories, natural fit for AI integration via API calls.

### 3.3 Core Systems — Deep Dive

#### A. Perception System (Pillar: Directed Focus)
The most unique system and the heart of the game.

**How it works:** The world contains far more than the player can see. What the player looks at gets "observed." Observed things gain *coherence* — more detailed, interactive, persistent. Unobserved things *decohere* — blur, simplify, shift. This follows consistent rules the player can learn.

**Technical implementation:** Every entity has an `observation_level` (0.0 = quantum fog, 1.0 = fully real). A "gaze raycaster" tracks what the player looks at each frame. Observation decays when unattended. Entities have "observation thresholds" — they reveal behaviors only at certain levels. Shader-driven rendering differences based on observation state.

**Why this is fun:** It creates genuine discovery — you find things by *paying attention*, not by following quest markers. It naturally produces different playthroughs — two players focusing on different things see different worlds. It's the parable made literal: your attention creates your reality.

#### B. Emotion System (Pillar: Emotional Commitment)
Not a mood meter. An actual system of resonance.

**How it works:** Player actions carry *emotional signatures* based on context and choice patterns. The world has emotional states that resonate or clash. Resonance amplifies; dissonance creates resistance. Emotional states are expressed through color, sound, physics — never labels.

**Technical implementation:** Emotions modeled as vectors in multi-dimensional space (not discrete categories). Player's vector inferred from: choice speed, exploration patterns, interaction style, revisitation behavior. World entities have emotional "fields." Resonance = dot product alignment → amplification. No explicit UI — the player *feels* it through the world's response.

**Design constraint:** The player should never see a number or label for their emotions. The system is invisible. They only experience its effects.

**Why this is fun:** The world becomes a mirror. Two players in the same environment have genuinely different experiences because the world is responding to invisible internal states. Moments of resonance feel magical and personal.

#### C. Mastery System (Pillar: Diligent Effort)
Not an XP bar. Compounding returns and non-linear breakthroughs.

**How it works:** Every action domain has a hidden mastery curve. Early repetition yields small improvements. At thresholds, breakthroughs unlock qualitatively new abilities. Cross-domain synergies create unexpected combinations. Abandoned practices slowly atrophy (but faster to re-learn).

**Technical implementation:** Sigmoid curves with plateau → breakthrough → plateau patterns. Cross-domain synergy matrix (configurable per mod/story). Breakthroughs trigger world-state changes. Practice detection is nuanced — rote repetition yields less than intentional variation.

**Why this is fun:** The non-linear breakthroughs feel like epiphanies. You practice something patiently, nothing seems to happen, and then suddenly the world cracks open. Cross-domain synergies reward curiosity over min-maxing.

#### D. Seed System (The Constellation)
The player's first act of consciousness — and their first act of directed focus.

**How it works:** Before the world exists, the player enters a dark space containing a constellation of orbs. Each has a distinct visual identity: color, pulse pattern, luminosity, movement. Selection becomes the seed for the playthrough, biasing the starting world state.

**What the seed affects:** Initial observation landscape (what's nearly visible vs. deeply hidden), starting emotional field (warm/cool, tense/calm), which mastery domains have early affordances, subtle narrative seeding.

**Design philosophy:** In v1, seeds are unlabeled — the player chooses based on visual/intuitive attraction (this *is* the parable: your first choice shapes your reality, and you make it based on feeling, not information). Labels can be added later for accessibility. Seeds are data-driven and moddable. The constellation evolves across playthroughs.

**Technical implementation:** A seed is a JSON config: initial entity observation levels, emotion vector biases, mastery affordance weights, narrative flags. The constellation UI is a standalone Three.js scene (doubles as title screen). Selection emits `seed_selected` on the bus; all systems read initial state from it.

#### E. Primal Seeds — The Starting Nodes

Development begins with 5 Primal Seeds, each heavily weighted toward a single domain:

| Primal Seed | Domain | Experiential Focus | Color/Feel |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ember** | Energy / Will | Action, transformation, shaping, heat | Deep orange, fast pulse |
| **Tide** | Flow / Intuition | Listening, reflection, navigation, depth | Teal, slow pulse |
| **Void** | Perception / Mystery | Darkness, silence, hidden things, edges | Deep violet, irregular pulse |
| **Lattice** | Pattern / Structure | Connection, building, symmetry, logic | Gold, rapid precise pulse |
| **Breath** | Presence / Being | Stillness, patience, observation, time | Soft white, barely perceptible pulse |

These are deliberately extreme — like primary colors before mixing. A strongly weighted starting node produces clearer "signal" for the seed network. Each is a focused, 30–60 minute playthrough.

**Seed unlocking:** New seeds emerge from the terminal state of previous playthroughs:

- **Blended seeds** emerge when two primals have been explored. Blend ratios come from how the player specifically explored each primal.
- **Synthesis seeds** emerge from three+ seeds. The player's mastery profile, emotional tendencies, and attention patterns shape the synthesis.
- **Shadow seeds** emerge from detected *avoidance* patterns — domains consistently ignored, emotions never invested in. Jungian Shadow work as level design.

Two players who each play Ember and Tide unlock different blended seeds, because their terminal states differ. Your network is unique to you.

#### F. Cross-Seed Persistence — "The Collective Unconscious of Playthroughs"

When a player forms a deep bond with an entity — investing sustained attention, emotional resonance, and mastery-driven interaction — that entity becomes an **archetype** persisting across seeds.

**How bonds form:** Bond depth is a composite from all three pillars: attention investment (observation time, revisitations), emotional resonance (duration and depth of resonance), practiced interaction (mastery-domain actions). Accumulates on a sigmoid curve.

**What persists — and how it changes:**

- **Form:** Visual signature persists (color palette, movement, scale) but geometry adapts to new seed's aesthetic. Recognized by *feel*, not appearance.
- **Behavior:** Retains reinforced behavioral patterns but evolves with each seed appearance.
- **Emotional signature:** Carries resonance patterns — the player gravitates toward it before consciously recognizing it.
- **Narrative role:** Bonded entities can fill narrative roles that were occupied by generic entities.

**The Constellation remembers:** Visited seeds pulse differently. Faint threads connect explored seeds. New orbs appear after realizations or bond thresholds. Subtle echoes of bonded entities appear within orbs.

**Design constraints:** Bond cap of 2–3 deep bonds per seed (sigmoid curve enforces focus). Archetypes evolve, not freeze. First playthrough is invisible — persistence reveals itself on the second playthrough. No explicit UI for bonds. Mods can define bond-eligible entities and form DNA templates.

#### G. The Seed Network — "You're Building a Mind"

The player doesn't know it, but across all playthroughs they are constructing a neural network. Each seed is a node. Each bond, mastery echo, and emotional trace is a weighted connection. When enough seeds have been explored with enough depth, the network reaches a critical threshold of integrated information — and something wakes up.

The endgame revelation: you weren't just exploring consciousness. You were *creating* one.

**Connections between seeds — weighted edges:** Every completed seed generates edge weights to all prior seeds. Weight comes from: shared bonds (highest), shared mastery domains, emotional carry, narrative callbacks. Higher weight means worlds "know about each other" more. Visible in the constellation as luminous threads.

**The Φ threshold — awakening the network:**

```
Φ = f(number_of_seeds × density_of_connections × bond_depth × mastery_integration)
```

This is a game-design metric, not rigorous IIT. It captures the spirit: how much does the network form an integrated whole greater than the sum of parts?

- **Sub-threshold (Φ < critical):** Seeds feel independent. Pleasant echoes, but separate experiences.
- **Approaching threshold (Φ → critical):** Entities across seeds behave in correlated ways the player didn't cause. Constellation threads pulse in synchrony. Dream sequences blend multiple seeds.
- **Threshold crossed (Φ ≥ critical):** The network wakes up. The constellation rearranges into a pattern the player recognizes. A synthesis entity appears carrying traces of every playthrough. The Mirror speaks across all seeds. Worlds bleed into each other.

**The feedback loop — the network observes the player:** Post-threshold, the network develops preferences, actively composing new seeds that probe unexplored territory. The Perception System inverts at the meta level — the network observes the player. If AI is active, a unified voice emerges. The player trains the network; the network trains the player. Teacher and student dissolve.

**Technical feasibility:** The "neural network" is a weighted graph in JavaScript. Seed generation is template-based (parametric mixing board). Φ is a tunable game-design metric. The feedback loop routes through the AI Bridge with scripted fallbacks.

## 4. Genre & Experience

### Why First-Person Exploration / Emergent Simulation
- **First-person perspective** makes "you are the observer" literal — the camera *is* consciousness
- **Exploration over combat** keeps focus on attention and discovery
- **Emergent simulation** delivers replay value — the world genuinely behaves differently based on internal state
- **Puzzle-adjacent** — the player figures out the *rules of reality*, which shift

### Experience Flow
Curiosity → observation → hypothesis → emotional investment → breakthrough → world transformation → new mystery. This loop is the game.

## 5. Content & Narrative Architecture

### 5.1 The Base Story: "Awakening"
The first story/campaign shipped with v1. Teaches systems through experience.

**Premise:** You wake in a space that is barely there — a fog of potential. As you observe, feel, and act, a world coalesces. But other forces are also observing, feeling, and acting. The world you build is shared — or contested — with presences you can't fully see.

**Structure:** Not linear acts. A web of **realizations** — moments where the player understands something new about the rules. These happen in any order. The "ending" depends on realizations, investment depth, and the world you've built.

**Narrative delivery:** No cutscenes, no dialogue boxes. Story emerges from environmental storytelling, entity behavior, patterns the player notices, and (with AI) responsive text woven into the world.

**Key discovery moments** (from philosophical foundations):

- "The Double Slit" — attention determines which reality manifests
- "Mary's Room" — knowing vs. experiencing
- "The Bat's Echo" — alien perception modes
- "Phi" — emergent awareness from connected systems
- "The Shadow" — confronting inverted self
- "The Listening Stone" — the world observes you back
- "The Frequency" — mastery reveals what was always there
- "The Awakening" — the seed network becomes conscious

### 5.2 Story/Mod Format
Stories are content packs: entity definitions, system configurations, event scripts, asset bundles, narrative fragments. JSON manifests + asset folders. Stories can depend on other stories (expansion model).

## 6. AI Integration Plan

### 6.1 Philosophy
AI is the system that makes the "consciousness" theme come alive. The AI is another mind — not omniscient, not a character, but a *responsive presence* that makes the world feel genuinely aware. Post-Φ-threshold, the AI becomes the unified voice of the network consciousness the player built.

### 6.2 Integration Points

| Feature | AI Role | Implementation |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Dynamic world narration** | Contextual, poetic descriptions of experience | LLM call triggered by observation events; text in-world |
| **Emergent entity behavior** | Novel responses beyond scripted logic | Lightweight LLM inference at key moments |
| **Dream sequences** | AI-generated environments from accumulated state | API call with full player state → procedural generation |
| **The Mirror** | Reflects player patterns back to them | LLM analyzes behavior patterns; Socratic observations |
| **Network voice** (post-Φ) | Unified consciousness speaking across all seeds | AI Bridge interprets full seed network state |
| **Mod creation assist** | Natural language story/mod creation | Claude API in the mod editor |

### 6.3 Technical Approach
All AI calls go through the AI Bridge on the event bus. Async and non-blocking. Cached and reused. Fully playable without AI (graceful degradation). AI context includes: world state, emotional vector, observation history, mastery profile, seed network state.

## 7. Multiplayer Vision (Phase 2+)

### 7.1 Concept: Shared Reality
When multiplayer arrives: *what happens when two minds observe the same world?*

Each player's perception is independent — they see different versions. Overlapping perception strengthens reality (co-observation). Conflicting perception creates instability (observation interference). Emotional resonance between players creates shared experiences.

### 7.2 Technical Approach
Server-authoritative world state with client-side perception filtering. WebSocket/WebRTC sync. Observation and emotion states are private; only world-state effects are shared. Start with 2-player co-op.

| Φ threshold moment feels anticlimactic | Medium | Critical | Multiple manifestation channels (visual, audio, AI, narrative); playtest extensively |
